# Quantum Computing with Cirq

Welcome to the repository for **"Quantum Computing with Cirq"**!

Explore the fascinating world of quantum computing through a series of interactive Jupyter notebooks. This collection covers various fundamental and advanced topics in quantum computing, all implemented using the Cirq library.

## 📚 Contents

### **1. Quantum Computing Fundamentals**

0. **[Representing Qubits](1.0_Representing_Qubits.html)**: Dive into the basics of qubits, the fundamental building blocks of quantum computing. Learn how qubits are represented and manipulated in quantum circuits.

1. **[Quantum Circuit](1.1_Quantum_Circuit.html)**: Understand the structure and components of a quantum circuit, and how they are used to perform quantum computations.

2. **[Quantum Gates (X, H, Z)](1.2_Quantum_Gates(X_H_Z).html)**: Explore the fundamental quantum gates—X, H, and Z—and their roles in quantum state manipulation and circuit design.

3. **[Entanglement](1.3_Entanglement.html)**: Discover the concept of entanglement and how it forms the basis for many quantum protocols and applications.

4. **[BB84 Protocol](1.4_BB84_Protocol.html)**: Learn about the BB84 quantum key distribution protocol and how it ensures secure communication.

5. **[Eve's Attacks on BB84 Protocol](1.5_Eve_Attacks_BB84_Protocol.html)**: Analyze potential security threats to the BB84 protocol and how Eve's attacks can compromise quantum communication.

### **2. Advanced Quantum Protocols and Algorithms**

1. **[Quantum Teleportation Protocol](2.1_Quantum_Teleportation_Protocol.html)**: Explore the quantum teleportation protocol, which allows the transfer of quantum states between distant qubits.

2. **[Superdense Coding Protocol](2.2_Superdense_Coding_Protocol.html)**: Study superdense coding, a technique for transmitting more information than classically possible using entanglement.

3. **[Noise and Fidelity](2.3_Noise_and_Fidelity.html)**: Investigate the impact of noise on quantum computations and how fidelity measures the accuracy of quantum states.

4. **[Noise in GHZ State](2.4_Noise_in_GHZ_State.html)**: Examine how noise affects the Greenberger-Horne-Zeilinger (GHZ) state, a key resource in quantum computing.

5. **[Error Correction](2.5_Error_Correction.html)**: Understand quantum error correction techniques that help in mitigating errors and improving the reliability of quantum computations.

6. **[Variational Algorithm (Minimize Cost)](2.6_Variational_Algorithm(Minimize_Cost).html)**: Delve into variational algorithms used in quantum computing to optimize cost functions and solve complex problems.

7. **[Quantum Network](2.7_Quantum_Network.html)**: Explore the concept of quantum networks and how they enable the development of quantum communication systems.

8. **[Grover's Algorithm](2.8_Grovers_Algorithm.html)**: Learn about Grover's search algorithm and its ability to search unsorted databases more efficiently than classical algorithms.

## 🚀 Getting Started

To get started, clone this repository and open the HTML files using a web browser. Each HTML file contains the content of the corresponding Jupyter notebook with detailed explanations and code to help you understand the concepts and their implementations.

Feel free to explore, experiment, and expand upon the work presented here. Happy quantum computing!
